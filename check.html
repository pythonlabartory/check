Journal of Transdisciplinary Research

The Journal of Transdisciplinary Research (JTR) is an academic journal established by Wachemo University to serve as a platform for the dissemination of high-quality scholarly work from the global scientific community. It is published biannually and operates as a non-profit international scientific journal committed to academic rigor, ethical publishing standards, and open intellectual exchange.

About the Journal

A university journal established to strengthen research dissemination, scholarly exchange, and institutional visibility.

The establishment of JTR reflects Wachemo University’s commitment to research, innovation, and academic excellence. The journal is intended to increase the visibility of faculty, staff, and student research, support a stronger culture of publication, and contribute to national and international scholarly dialogue.

Why the journal was established

Wachemo University has expanded teaching, postgraduate education, and research activity, yet the absence of a home-grown peer-reviewed journal has limited the visibility and accessibility of the university’s research outputs. JTR was conceived to address that gap and provide a credible publication platform for staff, students, and collaborators.

What the journal aims to advance

JTR seeks to foster dialogue, knowledge integration, and innovation across disciplines in order to address complex societal, environmental, and scientific challenges. It is designed to contribute to sustainable development, societal well-being, and the advancement of global knowledge.

What it contributes to the university

The journal is intended to strengthen institutional reputation, support academic staff in publication and promotion, increase student research dissemination, build partnerships, and improve the university’s contribution to evidence-based policy and practice.

Aim and Scope

Transdisciplinary in outlook, broad in coverage, and aligned with Wachemo University research priorities.

Objectives of JTR

The journal is intended to provide a credible platform for original and innovative scientific findings; encourage interdisciplinary and integrative approaches; promote the visibility of Wachemo University research; support Ethiopia’s and Africa’s development priorities; recognize indigenous knowledge systems when integrated with contemporary science; strengthen academic excellence; facilitate collaboration; and contribute to the development of early-career researchers and postgraduate students.

Designed for strong editorial management, responsible peer review, and timely publication.

JTR is structured to combine academic quality, editorial fairness, and an efficient publication pathway from submission to online release.

Editorial Workflow

1

Submission

Manuscripts are submitted for editorial consideration together with the required supporting materials.

2

Initial Editorial Assessment

Scope, formatting, originality, and ethical compliance are checked before peer review.

3

Double-Blind Review

Each manuscript is evaluated by at least two independent experts in the relevant field.

4

Revision and Decision

Authors respond to reviewer comments through minor or major revision where required.

5

Proof and Production

Accepted articles proceed to typesetting, proofreading, and author proof review.

6

Online Publication

Accepted articles are published online first after corrected proofs are received.

Governance and Editorial Structure

Built on formal oversight, editorial accountability, and a clear institutional structure.

The establishment document presents JTR as a journal with structured governance intended to ensure academic excellence, transparency, editorial integrity, and long-term sustainability.

Institutional Oversight

The journal is to be administered under the stewardship of the Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs, Research, Technology Transfer, and Community Service. The Research and Publication Directorate is assigned direct oversight of day-to-day operations, while the University Senate provides institutional support and policy endorsement.

Editorial Bodies and Roles

The journal framework includes an Advisory Board and an Editorial Board. The Editor-in-Chief holds overall responsibility for academic content and editorial standards. The governance structure also identifies the Managing Editor, Associate Editors, Guest Editors, Editorial Officer, Language Editor, and Secretary of the Editorial Team as key functions in journal management.

Editorial Principles

The journal policy emphasizes editorial independence, originality, confidentiality, ethical review, qualified reviewer selection, and constructive peer-review reporting. It also highlights diversity in reviewer and board composition, and the importance of fair, transparent, and academically grounded editorial decisions.

Indexing and Discoverability

JTR is presented as being in the process of registration and discoverability in services such as EJOL, AJOL, Google Scholar, CrossRef, Dimensions, WorldCat, and Index Copernicus, with future targets that include Scopus, Web of Science, and PubMed Central as the journal grows in output and reputation.
